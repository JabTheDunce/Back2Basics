Foundation Year

Introduction

If you are coming from college, the way you are taught in this course may be very different from the way you were taught in college, here are some things to expect in foundation year

Course Content

During foundation year, you will have a total of 12 courses to learn, these courses are split up into 4 super units, each super unit has a specific average needed for you to progress.

Super unit 1 consists of 3 math courses which are OB/C1, OB/C2 AND OF2. During semester 1, you will have to complete a test and depending on your results you will either be put into OB1 or OC2. If you have done Maths and Further Maths in A level, OB/C1 and OB/C2 will be very familiar, you will learn things like differentiation, integration, matrices and more. OF2 consists of two parts, vectors and probability. To progress, you will need a combined total average of 75%.

Super unit 2 consists of 3 science courses. You can choose which courses you want to do, you have the choice of doing two physics courses or one physics course and one chemistry course, the last course is computational thinking. The computational thinking course. To progress, you will need combined total average of 60%.

Super unit 3 consists of 3 maths courses which are ON1, OD2 and OJ2. To progress, you will need a combined total average of 70%.

Super unit 4 consists of ICT, Academic skills and the foundation year project. ICT is a simple course that will teach you the university computer rules. In Academic skills, you will learn how to produce an essay, produce a presentation and how to cite sources in the harvard style. In foundation year project, you will have to produce a website with a group, you wll be taught what makes a good website and will be taught how to write a website. To progress, you will need a combined total average of 65%

Blackboard

Blackbord is a website you will use to access your course content. Blackboard is where you will find all of your course's information, course notes, course videos and more. Blackboard is also where you will hand in your courseworks.

Lectures

Lectures take place in a physical location, you can see where a lecture takes place through your timetable in MyManchester. Lectures are review sessions, this means that the content discussed in the lecture will be about the week before's content. Lectures will clear up common misunderstandings, you may be given questions to do to test your understanding. This is also a chance for you to ask your lecturer any questions about the content.

Tutorials

For every course you have, you will have a tutorial. These tutorials will consist of smaller groups and a tutorial teacher. Every week you will be given a tutorial sheet to do before the tutorial, during the tutorial, the solution and answer for this will be shown. There may also be an in-tutorial sheet given if there is time. Tutorials and the tutorial sheets test your understanding of the week's content, and give you a chance to ask for help.

Drop in Sessions

If you are confused on a course topic and need to ask for help, there are drop-in sessions for each course everyweek, you can see this on your timetable. Drop in sessions are not mandatory, but I suggest going to these if you are not 100% on a topic.
